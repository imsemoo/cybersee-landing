External Threat Exposure

External Threats Are Targeting
Your Brand, People, Assets, and Data

Cyber risks no longer start only inside your network. Attackers operate across dark web forums, leaked credential markets, phishing domains, fake social accounts, rogue apps, and exposed public-facing assets. Without continuous external visibility, security teams may discover threats only after customers, employees, or attackers report them.

Leaked Credentials

Employee credentials exposed through breach dumps, stealer logs, and dark web marketplaces can lead to account takeover, fraud, and unauthorized access.

Phishing Domains

Lookalike domains and fake login pages can target your customers, employees, and partners before your team even knows they exist.

Brand Impersonation

Fake social accounts, impersonation pages, and fraudulent online profiles can damage trust and expose customers to scams.

Rogue Apps

Fake mobile apps using your brand can mislead users, collect sensitive data, and create reputational risk.

Exposed Assets

Unknown domains, subdomains, open services, expired certificates, and misconfigurations can give attackers an easy starting point.

Dark Web Chatter

Threat actor mentions, marketplace listings, and discussions involving your organization can signal early risk before an incident escalates.

The problem isn't that external threats exist.
The problem is that most teams discover them too late.
